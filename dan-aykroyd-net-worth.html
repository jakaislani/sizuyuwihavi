Dan Aykroyd’s Career

At the age of 17, Aykroyd made his television debut as Hart Pomerantz on the Canadian comedy-drama series “The Hart and Lorne Terrific Hour,” which aired from 1970 to 1971.

In addition, Aykroyd was eventually hired as a writer and performer for “Saturday Night Live.”

From 1975 through 1979, Aykroyd appeared on the program as Yortuk Festrunk, Beldar Conehead, and Elwood Blues, along with other roles.

Further, Aykroyd and fellow actor Belushi both starred in several movies, including “1941” in 1979 and “Neighbors” in 1989.

Moreover, Aykroyd had scheduled to co-star with Belushi in “Ghostbusters,” which Aykroyd wrote in the early 1980s.

Besides, Dan recreated the character of Peter Venkman for Bill Murray after Belushi died of a heroin overdose in 1982.

The 1984 movie made $295.7 million at the box office, spawning a sequel in 1989

Furthermore, an animated series  “The Real Ghostbusters,” which ran from 1986 to 1991, is also a sequel of the Ghostbuster.

Besides, Dan is expected to make his return in “Ghostbusters: Afterlife,” which will be at the cinema in 2021.

Similarly, Dan co-starred in “Trading Places” with his fellow actor Eddie Murphy in 1983, then created and acted in Spies Like Us and Dragnet in 1987.

In addition, the Canadian actor Aykroyd also played a lonely priest in the ABC sitcom “Soul Man” between 1997 to 1998.

In the 2000s, Dan featured in over a dozen blockbuster movies, including 50 First Dates, Driving Miss Daisy, and Tammy.

Awards & Achievements

The Canadian actor Dan was nominated for an Academy Award for his role in “Driving Miss Daisy“ in 1990.
